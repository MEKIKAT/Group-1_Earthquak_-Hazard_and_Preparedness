Evacuation Plan

  1. Imagine various scenarios
    • Start by asking some simple questions to determine the main threats you may encounter when developing an earthquake evacuation plan.
  1. Establish roles and responsibilities
    • When an earthquake emerges and you must evacuate, employees will look to their leaders for reassurance and guidance.
  1. Determine escape routes and nearest exits
    • When the shaking stops, wait in a secure location before checking to determine if you are wounded. You will be better equipped to assist others if you first look after yourself, and only then should you consider those close to you. Proceed with caution and keep an eye out for risks created by items that have fallen or shattered. Watch out for aftershocks.
  1. Create a communication plan
    • Include a list of people to contact in case of an emergency. Be sure to plan for the worst-case situation in any disaster.
  1. Follow up and reporting
    • Real-time communication and progress monitoring are required during an earthquake.
